# E-commerce SEO

Data source: On-page analysis (static + live fetch) — direct `curl` of live prerendered HTML/JSON-LD on www.containeronedepot.com, plus repo inspection of `src/app/product/[slug]/page.tsx`, `src/data/products.json`, `next.config.ts`, `src/lib/cart-context.tsx`, `src/app/cart/checkout/page.tsx`, and `src/app/api/reservation/route.ts`. DataForSEO Merchant tooling (`scripts/dataforseo_merchant.py`, `scripts/dataforseo_costs.py`) referenced in the agent brief is not present in this repo, so live marketplace/competitor positioning was not run rather than run unchecked — this dimension is not covered below.

Score: 64/100

## What Works

- The Critical image-schema bug from the prior audit is gone: `product.images.map((img) => img.startsWith("http") ? img : ...)` guards correctly, and live-fetched JSON-LD now emits valid, resolvable URLs (`https://www.containeronedepot.com/images/...jpg`, confirmed `200` on a sampled asset).
- All 208 products' images are self-hosted (`public/images/`, `/images/*` paths) — zero remaining references to the third-party `conexdepotshipping.com`/`cdn.shopify.com` hosts in `products.json`, and all referenced local files exist on disk (0 missing).
- `brand` is now correctly typed `{"@type": "Brand", "name": "Container One Depot"}` (was `Organization`), confirmed live — resolves the Merchant Center brand-type rejection.
- `Offer.shippingDetails` (OfferShippingDetails, US destination) and `Offer.hasMerchantReturnPolicy` (7-day finite window, buyer-paid return shipping) are both present in live JSON-LD — the latter is a new addition beyond the prior audit's scope and directly addresses a Merchant Center Shopping-ads requirement.
- Checkout, contact, and newsletter forms were migrated off `mailto:` links to real server-side submission (`/api/reservation`, `/api/contact`, `/api/newsletter` via Resend) — the checkout flow now actually delivers reservation requests instead of depending on the visitor having a configured mail client.
- Crawl infrastructure remains solid: sitemap and robots.txt both `200`, category sub-filter links (`?sub=Cargo+Worthy` etc.) still server-rendered and indexable.

## Findings

**No GTIN/MPN/SKU in Product schema** | High | Only 11 of 208 products (5%) carry a `sku` value; the rest emit no `sku`, `gtin`, `mpn`, or `identifier_exists: false`. Google Merchant Center requires a unique product identifier or an explicit `identifier_exists: false` declaration — most of this catalog will still be flagged "missing identifier" and blocked or demoted in Shopping listings. | Populate `sku` from internal unit/inventory IDs where they exist, and add `identifier_exists: false` plus reliable `brand`+`mpn` for custom/one-off units that genuinely have no GTIN. | Still Open (unchanged from prior audit)

**Condition variants (New/Used) still collapsed into one page/price** | High | 91 of 208 products (44%) are `type: "variable"` with `specs: [["Condition", "New, Used"]]` flattened into plain text, a single `regularPrice`, and no per-condition slug or Offer — identical to the prior audit's finding. Meanwhile other product lines (Cargo Worthy vs. Economy Grade) do get distinct slugs/pricing, so the inconsistency itself is unresolved. | Standardize on distinct slugs/Offers per condition, matching the pattern already used for grade variants elsewhere in the catalog. | Still Open (unchanged from prior audit)

**Image optimization pipeline disabled despite self-hosting fix** | Medium-High | `next.config.ts` still sets `images.unoptimized: true` with a comment explaining it was a stopgap for the old hotlinked-image quota problem; that comment is now stale since images are self-hosted, but the flag was never removed. Result: Next/Image serves raw files with no automatic WebP/AVIF re-encoding or responsive `srcset` resizing. Sampled local files vary widely in size — e.g. one product photo is 100×100px, another 269×238px, several sit at 745×600/800×533 — well under the ≥800px recommendation for several assets, and none are served as WebP. `remotePatterns` for the two now-unused external hosts are also still present (harmless but dead config). | Remove `unoptimized: true` now that images are same-origin (re-enabling Vercel's optimizer without hotlinking-driven quota risk), re-crawl the catalog for any images below 800px and re-source/upscale them, and drop the stale `remotePatterns` entries. | New (introduced/exposed by the self-hosting fix — the underlying config was never updated to match)

**Flat $1,000 deposit exceeds full unit price on 12 catalog items** | High | `depositFor()` in `src/lib/cart-context.tsx` now returns a flat `$1,000` regardless of item price (per commit 49fc49d). 12 of 208 products — mostly accessories (Shipping Container Lock Box $64.80, Vents $86.40, Roll Up Door Framing Kit $165.60, Window Shutter $193.54, Misc Surcharge $400, etc.) — are priced well below $1,000, so the checkout order summary and confirmation screen will show a deposit larger than the item's full price, which reads as broken to a buyer and undermines trust in the new checkout flow this audit was asked to verify. | Cap the deposit at the item's price (or a percentage of it, e.g. min(price, $1,000) or 50%) for low-ticket accessory SKUs, or exclude accessories from the reservation/deposit flow entirely and route them to a simple "Request a Quote" CTA instead. | New

**Offer availability is hardcoded, not tied to real stock** | Medium | Every product's `offers.availability` is a static `"https://schema.org/InStock"` string in `src/app/product/[slug]/page.tsx` — there is no stock/inventory field in `Product` type or `products.json` to reflect actual availability. For a live industrial-equipment reseller, this risks Merchant Center policy issues (misrepresenting availability) if units sell out or go on backorder without the feed reflecting it. | Add a real availability field to the product data model (in stock / out of stock / preorder / made-to-order) and drive both the on-page CTA and the schema `availability` value from it. | New (not explicitly called out in prior audit, surfaced during this pass)

**1 product has no images at all** | Low | One catalog entry in `products.json` has an empty `images` array, which renders a "NO IMAGE AVAILABLE" placeholder (`Gallery.tsx`) and would omit the schema `image` field for that listing, disqualifying it from Merchant Center/rich results. | Identify the affected SKU and source at least one product photo, or a category-representative placeholder image if a real photo isn't available. | New (not flagged in prior audit's sampling)

**No true faceted filtering (size/condition/grade) on category pages** | Medium | Unchanged from prior audit: category pages still only offer pre-built `?sub=` grouping links rather than combined size + condition + grade filtering. Server-rendered and indexable, so this remains a UX/conversion gap rather than a crawlability one. | Add a combined filter UI (client-side, with server-rendered fallback links preserved) once variant modeling (see above) is standardized. | Still Open (unchanged from prior audit)

**Marketplace/competitor positioning not assessed** | N/A | DataForSEO Merchant API tooling referenced in the agent brief (`scripts/dataforseo_merchant.py`, `scripts/dataforseo_costs.py`) is not present in this repository, so live Google Shopping placement and competitor pricing comparison could not be run. | Add the DataForSEO scripts/credentials to this repo (or run the comparison via an alternate tool) before the next audit cycle so pricing competitiveness can be verified against real marketplace medians rather than skipped. | Still Open (unchanged from prior audit — flagged, not run unchecked)

## Score rationale

64/100. The Critical schema-breaking bug and the third-party hotlinking risk from the prior audit (46/100) are both confirmed fixed live, plus two bonus wins (brand type, return policy) beyond what was asked. That's a real jump. But the score is held back by: two High-severity Merchant Center blockers carried over unresolved (missing identifiers, collapsed condition variants), a newly-introduced image-optimization regression (`unoptimized: true` never removed after self-hosting), and a new checkout logic bug where the flat $1,000 deposit exceeds the sale price on 12 low-ticket accessory items — a direct hit to the trustworthiness of the very checkout flow this audit was asked to validate.
